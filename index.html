Evidences for Evolution

Terminology

Different methods and scientific observations that help scientists determine how species evolved over time.

Comparative Anatomy

Definition

Comparative Anatomy is a type of evidence for evolution that looks at the similar anatomy between different species, and correlates them. This is a mostly an observation-based evidence for evolution.

Types of Structures

Vestigial: Structures that have apparent functions in one species, but not another. Evolution suggests that the newer species generally inherit vestigial structures from their ancestors, but stopped using them.

Homologous: Structures that look similar, but may have different functions. Evolution proposes that the structures used to have similar looks and functions, but changed as the organisms evolved and diverged.

Analagous: Structures that have the same functions, but that look different.

Examples

The picture shows an example of homologous structures between a horse and a dog. We see that they are shaped similarly, and have common bones. This proves that dogs and horses are closer together on the evolutionary timeline.

Flaws/Limitations

Analagous structures cannot be used as evidence for evolution because common uses are not as strong as an indicator as common structures. For example, both dogs and lizards have legs, but that does not mean that lizards evolved from dogs or vice versa.

Early Embryology

Definition

An evidence of evolution that says animals that look similar longer during their embryonic stages are more closely related.

Process

Biologists and Embryologists use a microscope to observe the embryos of different animals and find similarities and differences.

Flaws/Limitations

Species that have homologous structures after birth may be loosely related during their embryonic phases, and vice-versa.

These are the early embryoes of a human and a bird. Right now, they are very similar, but as they grow, they become more and more different. Species that stay more similar longer are more related.

Conclusion

Both of these evidences are not enough to prove evolution on their own. They need to be corroborated with other methodologies to holistically understand support the idea of evolution.
